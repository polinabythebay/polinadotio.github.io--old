Resources

A recommended list of books, essays,
videos, and podcasts

  • "The Brothers Karamazov"

    http://www.gutenberg.org/files/28054/28054-h/28054-h.html

    “The centripetal force on our planet is still fearfully strong, Alyosha. I have a longing for life, and I go on living in spite of logic."

  • "Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)"

    https://mitpress.mit.edu/sicp/full-text/book/book.html

    This book is dedicated, in respect and admiration, to the spirit that lives in the computer.

  • "What is Code?"

    http://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2015-paul-ford-what-is-code/

    A piece of digital journalism that attempts to bridge the gap between laypeople and programmers.

  • "Why do women try to get ahead by pulling men down?"

    https://medium.com/thoughts-on-society/why-do-women-try-to-get-ahead-by-pulling-men-down-a1345b36b91b

    An elevator metaphor for women in the tech industry.

  • "Three Miles"

    http://www.thisamericanlife.org/radio-archives/episode/550/three-miles

    A powerful episode about a program that brings together kids from two schools. One school is public and in the country’s poorest congressional district. The other is private and costs $43,000/year.

  • "Crucial Conversations"

    http://amzn.to/1JVKN8f

    How to communicate when the stakes are high.
